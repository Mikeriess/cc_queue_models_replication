# Study 3e — Multinomial-shape sensitivity for rank-binned NPS

Sweeps the *target* multinomial used by Study 3d's rank-binning while
holding the rest of the pipeline fixed. Mode held at 7.5 (symmetric
across bins 7 and 8); a single parameter `f` controls the mass placed
in the prioritised passive class.

## Background

Study 3d showed that rank-binning the predicted-NPS distribution to
match the empirical multinomial (n = 1,898) is the first intervention
in our study chain that breaks NPS ≡ LRTF at the paper's actual
calibration (intercept = 10.22). The advantage was real but small
(+0.15 pp organisation NPS over LRTF on average; +1.13 pp at ρ = 1.0).

Study 3d cannot disentangle three coexisting mechanisms: (1) explicit
priority for predicted passives, (2) a large bulk-tied class in bin 10,
(3) rank-preservation of the underlying prediction. Study 3e varies the
target multinomial along a single axis to isolate the first mechanism.

## Hypotheses

- **H1.** Organisation NPS for NPS_BINNED is non-monotone in `f`
  (inverted-U with an interior peak).
- **H2.** At `f = 0` (no mass in 7+8 → top priority class empty),
  NPS_BINNED still beats LRTF at high ρ. Value of rank-binning is
  not strictly about explicit passive priority.
- **H3.** At `f = 1` (all mass in 7+8 → every case ties at priority
  0.5), NPS_BINNED reduces to FCFS within sampling error.
- **H4.** `argmax_f organisation_NPS` is larger than the empirical
  share of bins 7+8 (0.145) — the empirical multinomial is not the
  operationally optimal target for NPS-priority.

## Target multinomial family

`f ∈ [0, 1]` is the total mass at bins {7, 8}. Mass is split evenly
between bins 7 and 8 (`p₇ = p₈ = f/2`). The remaining `1 − f` is
spread uniformly over the other 9 bins (0..6, 9..10), giving each one
`(1 − f)/9`.

Sweep grid: `f ∈ {0.00, 0.05, 0.10, 0.145, 0.20, 0.30, 0.50, 0.80, 1.00}`.

## Experimental design

| Factor | Levels |
|---|---|
| **Discipline** | FCFS, LRTF, SRTF, NPS, **NPS_BINNED** |
| **f** | 9 levels (above) — only NPS_BINNED |
| **ρ** | 0.0, 0.5, 1.0 (reduced from Study 3d's 5 levels) |
| **Sampling mode** | hard |
| **Agents** | 6 |
| **NPS intercept** | 10.22 (paper baseline) |
| **SLA** | none |
| **Replications** | 100 |

**Total runs:** 9·3·100 (NPS_BINNED) + 3·100·3 (LRTF/SRTF/NPS)
+ 3·100 (FCFS) = **3,900**.

## Sanity checks

1. Rank preservation per `f`: Spearman(`raw N̂PS`, `binned N̂PS`) = 1.000
2. Target conformance per `f`: per-rep histogram matches `p(f)` modulo
   one rounding leftover absorbed into bin 10.
3. `f = 0`: no case has `predicted_nps_binned ∈ {7, 8}`.
4. `f = 1`: every case has `predicted_nps_binned ∈ {7, 8}`. NPS_BINNED
   org NPS within 1 SE of FCFS at every ρ.
5. `f = 0.145`: NPS_BINNED reproduces Study 3d's empirical-multinomial
   cell within MC noise (defining `f = 0.145` here uses uniform tail,
   not the empirical tail — small expected deviation).
6. FCFS / LRTF / SRTF / NPS values match Study 3d cells at matching ρ.
